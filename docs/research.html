Research and other work

Overview

My work as a researcher started right after finishing my law degree, and my first publication came even earlier, as a product of my undergraduate dissertation.

Most of my published work is theoretical and qualitative. Jointly, for these positions I have conducted more than 50 interviews with criminal justice professionals, including judges of the Supreme Court of Chile. I have also done observation of court hearings and some ethnography. Additionally I have designed several grant proposals, especially during my time at CEJA. I highlight a proposal for IDRC about conflict resolution in which I designed a mixed methods research that was granted half a million dollars.

I hope my quantitative pieces of work will be published soon, but there is a library I created to structure unestructured data available at my Github.
